# Novelty Matrix (P0.4)

Each row is a dimension of the project's intended contribution. Columns: what the closest prior work does, the specific gap, and whether our synthetic P1 results support the contribution being real (evidenced), plausible, or refuted. This matrix is deliberately conservative: several intended contributions are **weakened or refuted** by our own synthetic results, and that is recorded here rather than hidden.

## Reading of the matrix

The project's *strong* novelty claims (N2 switching beats the average; N3 temporal order matters; N9 identifiability) are **not supported** by our own synthetic experiments — in the direction predicted by fast-switching averaging theory (N2, N3) and by the financial confound literature (N9). The *defensible* contributions are: a clean reproduction with causal controls (N1, N4, N5), a rigorous demotion of the naive connectivity gap (N6), a documented robustness envelope (N7), and a careful, explicitly-hedged translation framework (N8, N10).

**Consequence for the empirical stage:** the honest scientific value of a real-data study would be *confirmatory-negative or bounding* — testing whether the temporal/switching structure carries information beyond density, factors, and the average graph, with a strong prior (from both theory and these synthetic results) that it largely does **not**, and that identifiability is the binding constraint. This is reflected in the GO/NO-GO recommendation (see `STATUS.md`).
