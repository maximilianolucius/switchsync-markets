# Novelty Matrix (P0.4)

Each row is a dimension of the project's intended contribution. Columns: what the closest prior work does, the specific gap, and whether our synthetic P1 results support the contribution being real (evidenced), plausible, or refuted. This matrix is deliberately conservative: several intended contributions are **weakened or refuted** by our own synthetic results, and that is recorded here rather than hidden.

## Reading of the matrix (revised after the v1 audit)

Two of the strong claims that v1 recorded as **Refuted** (N2 switching beats static; N3 temporal order matters) are now marked **NOT ESTABLISHED**: the v1 experiments that "refuted" them were confounded (unpaired schedules, unequal horizons, a no-op dwell null, and the time-average rather than the best admissible static as comparator — audit D4/D5/D6), and Zhang–Strogatz (2021) show a genuine temporal advantage *can* exist at intermediate rates under a budget. So v1 neither refuted nor supported them; the questions are open and require the paired-schedule rerun (v2) and the separate intermediate-rate branch. N9 (identifiability) remains **weak/FAIL**, but its supporting `contraction_corr` evidence is **INVALID** (audit D10); the FAIL rests only on valid precision/recall.

The contributions that survive the audit intact are: a chaos-verified reproduction (N1, re-scoped to a calibrated demonstration G0B), the coverage/reachability distinction *as a concept* (N5 — though the v1 controls that "isolated" it are INVALID, audit D9, and must be rebuilt), the rigorous demotion of the naive connectivity gap (N6), and the hedged translation framework (N8, N10).

**Consequence for the empirical stage:** the earlier "strong prior that switching does not beat aggregate connectivity" was **over-stated** and is withdrawn. The honest position is that the mechanism's advantage over the best admissible static network is *untested*, identifiability is the binding constraint, and no empirical claim is warranted until the v2 paired reruns and the intermediate-rate branch have run. The GO/NO-GO recommendation in `STATUS.md` is correspondingly softened in the v1-audit addendum.
